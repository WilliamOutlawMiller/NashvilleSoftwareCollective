Constitutional Structure

The legal framework and procedural guidelines governing the Nashville Software Collective. This document outlines membership ranks, revenue distribution, and decision-making processes.

Article I: Membership Ranks

JUNIOR

Members defined by level of technical expertise, productivity, independence, and tenure.

Requirements for Promotion:

  1. Remain a member for at least one year
  2. Contribute to multiple projects
  3. Learn multiple new skills
  4. Lead a project to release
↓
Promotion: Apply for senior membership with 2/3 majority vote from all members
SENIOR

Long term members of the collective.

Privileges:

  • Choose between revenue sharing or salaried pay
  • Propose major financial decisions
  • Approve new member admissions
  • Initiate member removal proceedings

Responsibilities:

  • Contribute to and lead projects
  • Fill important roles across the collective
  • Develop junior members

Section 3.3: Day-to-Day Operations

No approval required for regular operational costs and day-to-day financial decisions. Senior members are expected to use their best judgement and get two approvals from other seniors when spending money on behalf of the collective.

Article IV: Member Removal Procedures

  • Actions more harmful than beneficial to the collective

Article V: Financial Transparency

All budgeting, salaries, and financial decisions are completely public within the collective. This transparency ensures all members understand resource allocation and can participate meaningfully in financial decisions.
