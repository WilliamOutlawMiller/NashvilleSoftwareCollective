What We Do

Contract Work

Members organize into project groups to accept and complete contracts. The collective uses its bargaining power and pooled resources to compete effectively in the contract market. All contract revenue is paid to the collective and shared equitably.

Software Development

Members develop and release software using collective server resources and talent. Revenue from non-contracted work using collective resources is paid to the collective, which covers operational costs including hosting and day-to-day operations.

Recreation & Community

The collective provides clean and accessible recreational spaces. Members host events, classes, workshops, and other community-building activities to support growth and collaboration.

How We're Structured

The collective operates with a simple, flat structure: two roles, no job titles, no management layer, and complete financial transparency.

Junior Members

New members are required to join at the Junior level and complete the following requirements: remain 1 year at the collective, contribute to multiple projects, learn new skills, and lead a project to release. After meeting these requirements, juniors may apply for senior membership with a 2/3 vote approval.

Senior Members

Long term members who can choose between revenue sharing and salaried pay. Seniors contribute to and lead projects, fill important roles across the collective, and help develop junior members.

Learn More About Our Structure

Complete Financial Transparency

All budgets, salaries, and financial decisions are completely public within the collective. This transparency ensures all members understand how resources are allocated and can participate meaningfully in financial decisions.

Democratic Decision-Making

Financial review meetings are held twice a year. All significant financial decisions require a 2/3 majority vote to pass.

Minor Financial Decisions

Financial Decisions under $1000 dollars can be made by a senior with approval from two other seniors.

Revenue Sharing

Senior members can choose proportional revenue sharing based on the collective's total revenue from contracts, software sales, and other income streams.

Public Salaries

All salaries and raises are completely public and transparent. Members can choose between revenue sharing and salaried positions, with conversions decided democratically.

Branches

The Nashville Software Collective is an outline, not a single company. Currently operating in Nashville, the collective welcomes new branches in other cities. Branches operate independently with revenue sharing extending to their immediate branch. Collectives are free to merge when ready, with new constitutions drafted to maintain nuanced local decision-making.
